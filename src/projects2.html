TV Remote Jammer Circuit

Introduction

A television remote uses light to carry signals from the remote to the television. The LED on the remote emits invisible infrared light that correspond specific binary codes. These binary codes contain commands like power on, volume up, or channel change.

The idea behind the TV remote control jammer is sending a constant IR pulse with the carrier frequency of the transmitter and confusing the infrared receiver in TV. Hence, the result will be non-accepted signal from the receiver and therefore no action will be taken.

Components

  1. BC547B:NPN transistor
  2. NE555 :555 Timer
  3. 1N4148:Diode
  4. Resistor-470ohm, 1.85k ohm, 5.6ohm
  5. Capacitor-10nF
  6. DC voltage source-9V
  7. LED
  8. Connecting Wires

Working Principle

Most of the TV remotes have 38KHz operating frequency. A flood of IR beams in the same frequency can easily confuse the TV receiver and this is the operating principle of our jammer. The circuit is nothing but an astable multivibrator based on NE555 TIMER IC.

The output of NE555 is amplified using a NPN transistor to drive the IR LED. Circuit can be adjusted to exactly match the jammer’s frequency to the remote’s frequency. This adjustment is very essential for effective jamming. Not only TV remotes, but any IR remotes operating in the 38KHz frequency region can be also jammed by using this circuit. In the circuit, TRIG and THRS pins are shorted to allow the triggering after every timing cycle. These two pins are grounded through the capacitor. RST pin is connected to supply to avoid sudden resets.

Conclusion
